Q: What's the secret to your success?
A: I'm rational. That's the answer. I'm rational.

Q: Can you give us some advice on how to find a great business at a fair price?
A: If it were that easy, everybody would be rich. It's not supposed to be easy. Anyone who finds it easy is stupid.

Q: How should we think about diversification?
A: The whole secret of investment is to find places where it's safe and wise to non-diversify. It's just that simple. Diversification is for the know-nothing investor; it's not for the professional.

Q: What do you think about EBITDA as a measure of earnings?
A: Every time you hear "EBITDA," just substitute it with "bullshit earnings."

Q: How do you avoid making big mistakes in life and business?
A: It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent. Invert, always invert. All I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there.

Q: What should a young person look for in a career?
A: Three rules. Don't sell anything you wouldn't buy yourself. Don't work for anyone you don't respect and admire. Work only with people you enjoy. I have been incredibly fortunate in my life: with Warren Buffett I had all three.

Q: Do you have any thoughts on the growing use of leverage?
A: There are only three ways a smart man can go broke: liquor, ladies, and leverage. Now the truth is, the first two he just added because they started with L — it's leverage.
